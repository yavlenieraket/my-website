about

Ekaterina Iakovleva studies ocean and island relational, ancient and contemporary landscapes, focusing on ecology and environmental inequality, with additional analysis of economic and colonial violence. She conducts research using anthropological, ethnographical and post-anthropological methods, field philosophy, qualitative and quantitative methods for image analysis, ethnographic work, spatial analysis and data modelling. She holds a Master’s degree in Anthropology, Digital Humanities and Philosophy from Paris Sciences et Lettres (PSL), completed in 2024. Ekaterina also curates exhibitions and artist residencies, working with decolonial artists and exploring themes of migration and hospitality.
